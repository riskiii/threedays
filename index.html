The Band

The group was originally Groundswell with five members in 1992. Three Days Grace was formed five years later as a trio in 1997 with their first album titled "Three Days Grace" in 2003. With their first album they ranked #9 on the Billboard's Top Canadian Albums. In the following year they were nominated for the Best New Artist for Billboard and Juno. In 2006 They climbed to #5 in The Billboard 200 and #2 in the Top Canadian Albums with "ONE-X". It was in the following year that they made #1 in the Billboard's Rock Artist of the year.

The Members

Matt Walst, the younger brother to the band's bass guitarist, Brad Walst, joined the band as the lead vocalist when Adam Gontier left for health related reasons. Matt was born December 18, 1982 making his age 31 and the youngest member. He got his first start when he founded the band "My Darkest Days" as the lead vocalist.

Brad Walst the older brother to Matt Walst is the bass guitarist and was with the band when it was first created back in 1997. Brad was born on February 16, 1977 putting his age at 37. Brad is married to Rhonda Walst and has two children, James and Grayden Walst.

Barry Stock joined the band in 2003 five years after Three Days Grace was orginally formed by Brad Walst, Neil Sanderson, and Adam Gontier. Barry was born on April 24, 1974 making him the odest member of the band at 40 years old. He is the band's lead guitarist and is married to Heather Stock.

Neil Sandersonis the band's drummer and backing vocalist. Neil's first band he played for was the Christian rock band "Thousand Foot Krutch" from 1995-1997 when he then co-founded Three Days Grace. Born on December 17, 1978 he is now 35 years old and is married to Janin Sanderson.

Adam Gontier was the co-founder and lead vocalist for Three Days Grace until 2013 when he left the band for health issues and was replaced by Matt Walst. Adam was born on May 25, 1978 and is married to Naomi Faith Brewer. Adam also play in a film called "Raise Your Voice" along with band members Brad, Barry, and Neil.
